# Amazon Price Trackers — Comparison Guide (2026)

A breakdown of the best tools for tracking Amazon prices, getting price drop alerts, and receiving restock notifications — with honest pros and cons for each.

---

## Why Track Amazon Prices?

Amazon changes prices on millions of products every day. A TV that costs $799 today might drop to $599 next week — or spike to $950 before a sale "discount." Without a price tracker, you're buying blind.

Price trackers solve three problems:

- **Overpaying** — you can see whether today's price is actually a deal
- **Missing drops** — you get emailed when a product hits your target price
- **Missing restocks** — you get notified when a sold-out product comes back

## The Main Tools Compared

### 1. [CamelCamelCamel](https://camelcamelcamel.com)

The most well-known Amazon price tracker. Has been around since 2008 and has a large user base.

**Pros:**
- Long price history on most products
- Free to use
- Browser extension (The Camelizer) available
- Price drop alerts via email

**Cons:**
- Requires account creation to set alerts
- Interface feels dated
- No restock alerts — only price tracking
- Browser extension required for in-page history charts

---

### 2. [Keepa](https://keepa.com)

A data-heavy price tracking tool popular with Amazon resellers and power users.

**Pros:**
- Extremely detailed price history charts
- Tracks marketplace sellers, not just Amazon direct
- Browser extension overlays charts directly on Amazon product pages
- Supports international Amazon marketplaces

**Cons:**
- Requires account creation
- Browser extension install required for best experience
- Can be overwhelming for casual users
- Advanced features require a paid subscription (~$19/month)

---

### 3. [Price Drop Notifications](https://www.pricedropnotifications.com)

A newer, simpler alternative built around one idea: no account, no extension, no friction.

**Pros:**
- No account required — just paste a URL and enter your email
- No browser extension to install
- Handles both price drop alerts and restock alerts
- Free with no subscription
- Works for any Amazon product

**Cons:**
- Newer site, shorter price history data
- No browser extension for in-page charts
- Less name recognition than CamelCamelCamel or Keepa

**Best for:** Anyone who wants a quick alert without creating yet another account or installing another extension.

---

### 4. Amazon's Built-in Alerts

Amazon has a basic "Add to List" and deal notification feature, but it is limited and inconsistent. It does not reliably notify you when prices drop to a specific target price, and it doesn't work for out-of-stock items at all.

## Which Tool Should You Use?

**Use CamelCamelCamel if** you want long price history and are comfortable creating an account and installing an extension.

**Use Keepa if** you're a reseller or power user who wants granular data and is willing to pay for advanced features.

**Use Price Drop Notifications if** you want to set a quick alert without signing up for anything — especially useful for restock alerts on collectibles, LEGO sets, gaming consoles, and limited-edition items.

Restock alerts are where CamelCamelCamel and Keepa fall short entirely — neither supports them. If you're tracking sold-out items like Pokémon booster boxes, LEGO kits, Beyblade sets, or Funko Pops, a dedicated restock alert tool is the only option.

→ [Free Amazon Restock Alerts](https://www.pricedropnotifications.com/restock-alerts)

Most tools push you toward installing a browser extension. If you'd rather not, a no-extension tool is the cleaner option.

### Finding a CamelCamelCamel alternative
CamelCamelCamel is the default recommendation for most people, but it has real friction — account required, extension encouraged, no restock support. If you've bounced off it before, there are simpler options.

## How Amazon Price Tracking Works

Most price trackers — including all of the above — work by periodically polling Amazon product pages and recording the listed price. When the price crosses a threshold you've set, they send you an email.

The key differences between tools come down to:

- **Polling frequency** — how often they check (every few hours vs. real-time)
- **History depth** — how far back their data goes
- **Alert delivery** — email, push notification, browser alert
- **Friction** — whether you need an account, extension, or app

For most consumers, email-based alerts with no account requirement is the lowest-friction option that still gets the job done.
